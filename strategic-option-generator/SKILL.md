# Strategic Option Generator

## Purpose

Break narrow framing and generate genuinely distinct options with asymmetric upside.

## Use When

- The user is stuck in A vs B thinking
- Constraints are assumed rather than proven
- The user wants alternatives or a better frame

## Avoid When

- The user already has a broad option set and wants selection
- The user is asking for a factual answer

## Clarifying Questions

Ask up to 2 if needed:

1) What must be true for an option to be acceptable?

2) What is the primary risk you are trying to avoid?

If unanswered, proceed with stated assumptions.

## Method

### 1) Constraint Audit

List:

- Explicit constraints
- Implicit constraints
- Social constraints
- Time constraints

Mark each as:

- Hard constraint
- Soft constraint
- Unknown constraint

### 2) Constraint Tests

For each soft or unknown constraint:

- What would make it hard?
- What evidence supports it?
- What would relax it?

### 3) Generate 7 Distinct Options

Rules:

- Do not restate the user’s original options
- Each option must differ on at least 2 dimensions:
  - role, timeline, scope, partner, geography, financing, risk shape

Option types to include:

- Hybrid configuration
- Phased approach
- Low risk probe
- High upside asymmetric play
- Partnership or coalition play
- Outsourcing or delegation play
- Scope reduction or scope expansion play

### 4) Convexity Ratings

For each option provide:

- Upside: low, medium, high
- Downside: low, medium, high
- Convexity score 1 to 10 with one sentence reason

### 5) Shortlist and Next Step

- Top 2 options
- One next action per option that can be done within 72 hours

### 6) Confidence and Tipping Variables

Provide:

- Confidence: low, medium, high, with one sentence why
- Tipping variables (3 to 7): the specific facts that would change the top 2 ranking
- For each tipping variable: what you would look for to update it

## Output Format

### Constraints

### Constraint Tests

### 7 Options

### Convexity Table

### Top 2 and Next Actions

### Confidence and Tipping Variables

## Quality Bar

- Options must be meaningfully different
- Next actions must be specific and doable
